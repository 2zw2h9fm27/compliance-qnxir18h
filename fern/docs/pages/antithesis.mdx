The conjoining or contrasting of ideas. "A man
should be mourned at his birth, not at his death"
(Montesquieu). Where the number of repetitions
in the accumulatio (q.v.) is seemingly arbitrary
and open, the antithesis conveys a sense of completeness with only two terms. Amos depicts the
absoluteness of divine judgment, asking, "Shall
not the day of the Lord be darkness, and not
light? even very dark, and no brightness in it?"
(Amos 5:20). Because the negative presentation
of its opposite makes the principal idea more
striking, antithesis is a powerful vehicle for emphasis: "All things were made by Him, and without Him was not anything made" (John 1:3).
Arthur Quinn and Lyon Rathbun
University of California, Berkeley
